# GTM.md

## Exact target user

Not "startups." The specific person: **an engineering manager or CTO at a 10–50 person B2B SaaS startup, Series A or bootstrapped, who has signed off on at least 3 AI tool subscriptions in the last 12 months without a formal procurement process.** They approved Cursor for the devs, ChatGPT Team because someone asked, and Claude because it was $20 and "why not." Now it's $800/month in AI subscriptions and they have a vague feeling it's messy but no time to audit it.

Secondary: **a technical founder running a 2–8 person team** who handles their own tooling decisions and uses a business credit card for SaaS. They see the monthly total, feel it's high, but can't point to what to cut.

## Getting the first 100 users in 30 days, $0 budget

**Week 1 — Seed with real audits:**
Run audits for 10 founders in my network. Screenshot the results. Post results (anonymised) on X: "Audited a 12-person startup's AI stack. Found $640/mo in waste in 4 minutes. Here's what they were doing wrong: [thread]." Tag the specific tools mentioned. This works because it's specific, has real numbers, and names tools people recognise.

**Week 2 — Hacker News Show HN:**
Post "Show HN: SpendWise – free AI spend auditor for startup teams." HN rewards tools that are genuinely useful, free, and fast. The demo link must work perfectly. Target weekday morning US time. Expect 50–200 upvotes if the tool is sharp; that alone can drive 500+ visitors.

**Week 3 — Reddit threads:**
Find active threads on r/ExperiencedDevs and r/cscareerquestions where people are debating AI tools. Add a genuine, helpful comment: "We built a free tool that audits this exact question — it compares Cursor vs Copilot based on team size and use case." Don't spam. Pick 5 threads with >50 comments where the question is directly relevant.

**Week 4 — Indie Hacker community post:**
"I built a free AI spend auditor this week. Here's what I learned talking to 20 startup founders about their AI tool bills." This format (build + learnings) gets traction because it's educational, not promotional.

## The unfair distribution channel

Credex already has customers — startups that bought AI credits through them. Credex can seed SpendWise by running audits on their existing customer base proactively. "We noticed you're paying for X and Y — here's your spend audit." This is a warm email to people who already trust Credex, from a company with real context on their AI spend. No cold outreach required. First 50 high-quality leads come from people who already have a reason to talk to Credex.
