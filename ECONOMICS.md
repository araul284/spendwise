# ECONOMICS.md

## What a converted lead is worth to Credex

A startup spending $800/mo on AI tools and overspending by $300/mo is exactly the lead Credex wants. If Credex sells them $500/mo in discounted credits (replacing $700 of retail spend), and Credex's margin on sourced credits is ~25%, that's **$125/mo gross profit per customer**, or **$1,500/year per retained customer**.

Assume 6-month average retention at this stage (early product, competitive market): **LTV ≈ $750 per converted customer.**

High-spend customers ($2,000+/mo API bills) are more valuable: LTV likely $3,000–6,000 if they stay 12 months. These are the >$500/mo savings cases we surface prominently with the Credex CTA.

## Conversion funnel

```
Landing page visitor
  → 25% complete the audit form          (audit completed)
  → 25% of completers capture email      (lead captured)
  → 15% of email leads book consultation (qualified lead)
  → 40% of consultations convert to sale (customer)

Effective: 1,000 visitors → 250 audits → 62 emails → 9 consultations → 4 customers
```

At $750 LTV per customer: **1,000 visitors → $3,000 in Credex revenue.**

Revenue per visitor: $3.00. At $0 CAC (organic), every visit is pure upside.

## Break-even on paid budget

If we run paid ads: CAC via Twitter ads ≈ $300 per booking, 40% close → $750 per customer.
Margin per customer: $750 LTV − $750 CAC = $0. **Paid ads don't work at current LTV estimates.**

This reinforces the organic-first strategy. Paid ads only become viable if LTV increases (longer retention, larger credits purchased) or we can cut CAC below $300.

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 months

$1M ARR = ~$83k/month in Credex revenue = ~$83k/month gross profit at ~25% margin = 555 active customers paying ~$150/mo average.

**What has to be true:**

1. **SpendWise drives 50+ qualified consultations per month by month 6.** Requires ~5,500 visitors/month → 1,375 audits → 340 email leads → 51 consultations. Achievable with 1 HN post/month and consistent X presence.

2. **Consultation → sale rate holds at 40%.** Means 20 new customers/month by month 6.

3. **Churn stays below 8%/month.** At 20 new/month and 8% churn: steady state ≈ 250 customers by month 12. Need to double new customer rate to 40/month in months 12–18.

4. **Average credits purchased grows to $500/mo.** Early customers may start at $200; as they see ROI they expand. Credex needs an expansion motion — usage-based upsell when credits run low.

5. **Retention improves to 12+ months.** If credits genuinely save money, there's no reason to churn. The risk is startups pivoting away from the specific tools Credex supplies.

**The number that triggers a pivot:** If consultation → sale rate drops below 20% after 50+ consultations, the problem is either (a) the audit is surfacing the wrong leads, or (b) Credex's credit selection doesn't match what the audits surface. Investigate and fix the tool-credit pairing before scaling further.
